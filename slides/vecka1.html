Pair-programming

Pair programming (PP) involves two people sitting side by side, using only one computer and working collaboratively on the same design, algorithm, code, or test. One is the "driver," who is responsible for designing, typing the code, and having control over the shared resource (e.g., computer, mouse, and keyboard). The second is the "navigator" or "observer," who has responsibility for observing how the driver works in order to detect errors and offer ideas in solving a problem. Throughout their work, pairs typically alternate their roles after a certain duration.

N. Salleh, E. Mendes and J. Grundy, "Empirical Studies of Pair Programming for CS/SE Teaching in Higher Education: A Systematic Literature Review," in IEEE Transactions on Software Engineering, vol. 37, no. 4, pp. 509-525, July-Aug. 2011, doi: 10.1109/TSE.2010.59.

da Silva Estácio, B.J. and Prikladnicki, R., 2015. Distributed pair programming: A systematic literature review. Information and Software Technology, 63, pp.1-10.
